Malaysian Amateur Radio Bands Guide

Complete guide to amateur radio frequency bands available in Malaysia. Covers VHF, UHF, and HF allocations, repeater offsets, band plans, and operating conventions.

Overview of Malaysian Amateur Radio Bands

Malaysian amateur radio operators, licensed by the Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC), have access to several frequency bands across the HF, VHF, and UHF spectrum. The most commonly used bands for local communication are the 2-meter VHF band (144–148 MHz) and the 70-centimeter UHF band (430–440 MHz). These bands support simplex operation, repeater operation, digital modes like DMR, and packet radio including APRS.

VHF Band — 2 Meters (144–148 MHz)

The 2-meter band is the most popular amateur radio band in Malaysia for local and regional voice communication. It supports simplex (direct radio-to-radio) and repeater operation.

PMR446 (446.000–446.200 MHz) — License-Free

While not technically an amateur radio band, PMR446 is included in our database because many Malaysian ham radio operators also use PMR446 for license-free personal communication. PMR446 has 16 channels from 446.00625 to 446.19375 MHz with 0.5W maximum power. See our PMR446 guide for full details.

HF Bands (Shortwave)

Malaysian amateur radio operators with full licenses also have access to HF (High Frequency) bands for long-distance (DX) communication. HF signals bounce off the ionosphere, enabling worldwide communication.

Repeater Operation in Malaysia

Repeaters are automated relay stations that receive on one frequency and retransmit on another, extending the communication range of mobile and handheld radios. Malaysia has an extensive network of repeaters covering all states, typically located on mountain tops and high buildings.

How to Use a Repeater

  • Program the repeater's output frequency into your radio
  • Set the correct offset (0.6 MHz for VHF, 5.0 MHz for UHF)
  • Set the correct CTCSS tone (e.g., 88.5 Hz, 203.5 Hz)
  • Press PTT to transmit — your radio shifts to the input frequency automatically
  • Listen for the repeater's beep (courtesy tone) before responding
  • Pause between transmissions to allow others to break in

Digital Modes in Malaysia

DMR (Digital Mobile Radio)

Several Malaysian repeaters support DMR digital mode, including 9M2RKL-DMR (Kuala Lumpur), 9M4RXC-DMR (Penang), and 9M2RBB-DMR (Penang). These repeaters are connected to the Brandmeister Network, enabling worldwide digital voice communication through Talk Groups.

APRS (Automatic Packet Reporting System)

APRS operates on 144.390 MHz in Malaysia. It's used for real-time position reporting, weather station data, and short text messages. APRS digipeaters and iGates relay packets across the network.

ASTRA Transnasional Linking

Several Malaysian repeaters are part of the ASTRA Transnasional linking network, which connects repeaters across different states via the internet or RF links, allowing operators to communicate over very long distances through the linked system.

CTCSS and DCS Tones

Malaysian repeaters use CTCSS (Continuous Tone-Coded Squelch System) or DCS (Digital-Coded Squelch) tones to control access. You must program the correct tone to activate a repeater. Common CTCSS tones used in Malaysia:

Licensing in Malaysia

To transmit on amateur radio bands in Malaysia, you need:

  • Radio Amateurs Examination (RAE) — pass the written exam
  • Amateur Radio Operating Certificate (AROC) — issued by MCMC
  • Station License — annual fee, issued by MCMC
  • Callsign — 9M2 (full) or 9W2 (intermediate) prefix

For more details, see our FAQ page or visit the MARTS (Malaysian Amateur Radio Transmitters' Society) website. For license-free communication, see our PMR446 guide.
